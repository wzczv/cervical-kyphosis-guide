About Cervical Curve Guide

Cervical Curve Guide is a multilingual health education project about cervical kyphosis, straightened or flattened cervical curve findings, hand numbness, arm pain, conservative rehabilitation, and sport-specific neck loading.

Mission

The site is designed to help readers interpret imaging language without panic, recognize neurological warning signs, and understand conservative care options in a measured, source-backed way.

Editorial stance

We avoid promising that exercises can restore the cervical curve, cure nerve pain, or replace clinical evaluation. The site emphasizes red flags, differential diagnosis, symptom response, and gradual exposure.

Authors and review status

Content is currently written by the Cervical Curve Guide editorial team. The site is source-backed and reviewed for conservative wording, but it has not yet completed independent review by a named licensed clinician. See the authors page and medical review policy for the current standard and future reviewer requirements.

Languages

The current site supports simplified Chinese, English, Japanese, and Spanish. Translations are adapted for natural reading in each language rather than copied mechanically word for word.

Independence

This version does not accept sponsorships, paid placements, affiliate commissions, or advertising. YouTube links are included as visual references after the on-site safety guidance, not as substitutes for the site's own educational content.

Medical boundary

The site is not medical advice, diagnosis, physical therapy prescription, or return-to-sport clearance. People with new weakness, spreading numbness, gait changes, bowel or bladder symptoms, major trauma, fever, cancer history, or rapidly worsening symptoms should seek prompt medical care.
